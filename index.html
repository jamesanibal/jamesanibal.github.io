Autonomous Robot

May 2016 - August 2016

Every year, second year Engineering Physics students participate in a design competition. For my year, our challenge was to make an autonomous robot that could pick up stuffed animals that were emitting an IR signal, and drop them off at a specified location. Our robot - the Bad Apple, was built from raw materials using both the water jet cutter and the laser cutter. The electrical circuits were soldered onto PCBs and installed within our robot. I wrote the code for the signal processing and the maneuvering of the arm/claw (Arduino microcontroller, C++). I also helped build the signal processing circuits.

My three teammates and I won third place in the competition. Here is the competition page and our project's webpage.

Datasets Organizer

January 2016 - April 2016

Because of my interest in data, I wanted to create a script that could effectively organize and optimize data analysis. I used the data provided on the Cancer Genomic Data Server (CGDS) by cBioPortal. The script I wrote utilizes the CGDS-R package to organize cancer data sets. In particular, the script allows researchers to input a list of genes and a particular cancer of interest; the output is an organized CSV file with all the relevant cancer studies, mutations, and copy number alterations related to the list of genes and the specified cancer.

Feel free to send me an email if you would like to use the script.

Machine Learning

February 2017 - Ongoing

Efficacy rates of Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) are in the range of 50-55% response, and rTMS has become a first-line treatment for treatment-resistant depression (TRD). However, efforts to predict treatment response are underway, using a variety of neuroimaging, physiological parameters. At the NINET Lab, my goal is to investigate the predictive features of detailed symptom profiles in TRD. My project aims to use methods of machine learning to predict how certain predictors on different depression rating scales (HRSD and IDS) will affect a patientâ€™s response to Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) treatment. My analysis is performed in R, using the caret package.
