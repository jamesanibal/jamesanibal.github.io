ABOUT

I am a PhD student at the University of Oxford's Clinical Health Informatics Lab. I have previously completed a B.A.Sc. in Engineering Physics (UBC, 2019) and a M.Sc. in Bioinformatics (UBC, 2020). RESEARCH PROJECTS

DIGITAL HISTOPATHOLOGY

My research involves developing and analyzing a convolutional neural network that automates the detection of therapeutically informative genomic events directly from images of biopsied tumour tissue. This project would cut-down the time and expenditure needed for detailed pathology and genomic analyses, and would help identify prognostic genomic events in rare and advanced forms of cancer.

CANCER GENOMICS

Using an autoencoder model, I wanted to determine which genes carry the most information for discriminating between different cancer types. I configured the model using the Tensorflow framework and trained the model on large-scale transcriptomic data.

I presented a poster at the International Conference of Physics Students and won 3rd place. Here is an article UBC published about the conference.

TMS TREATMENT RESPONSE

I investigated the predictive features of detailed symptom profiles in treatment resistant depression. I trained four machine learning models to predict how certain predictors on different depression rating scales (HRSD and IDS) affect a patient’s response to transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) treatment.

Here is an article UBC published about my project.
